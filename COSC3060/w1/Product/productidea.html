Practice Challenge

In Vietnam, the educational landscape sees a significant influx of domestic students, with over 11 million students aged between 18 and 24 years actively pursuing their studies. This young demographic often finds itself in need of suitable living arrangements, gravitating towards shared living spaces or more economically viable accommodation options. Currently, the bustling cities of Vietnam are home to approximately 30,000 rooming houses, designed to cater to the accommodation needs of students and migrant workers alike. Amidst this backdrop, a staggering 6.6 million students are actively seeking shared accommodation, underscoring the pressing demand for affordable and conducive living environments.
The Practice Challenge invites innovative minds to develop a technological solution aimed at enhancing the rental experience for students, landlords, community organizations, or governmental bodies involved in the sphere of shared accommodation or rooming houses. This initiative seeks to streamline the process, making it more efficient, accessible, and beneficial for all parties involved in the Vietnamese context.
